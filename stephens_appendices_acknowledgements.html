Acknowledgements

The editors would like to thank The Ohio State University Libraries for permission to publish this edition of selected letters from the Ivan S. Gilbert Collection of Stephens Family Travel Letters and Ephemera and to incorporate images of the manuscript held in the Rare Books and Manuscript Library. Dr. Geoffrey D. Smith, former Head of Rare Books and Manuscripts, introduced Professor Ulman to the Stephens Family collection and suggested that he and his students edit the materials. Lisa Iacobellis, Assistant Curator of Rare Books and Manuscripts, scanned the manuscript pages to exacting standards.

This electronic textual edition also depends on technical support provided by a number of organizations and individuals at The Ohio State University. The College of Arts and Sciences hosted the original Web site and XML publishing system used to develop the edition.

High-resolution images of the manuscipt pages were originally housed on the Colleges of the Arts and Sciences Media Manager application, which allowed the edition to provide users with zoomable views and numerous detailed views of the manuscript pages on the fly, all from archived master images. George Abraham, Web Applications Developer, and Allen Coleman, Digital Library Developer, offered frequent advice and encouragement for our custom applications of the Media Manager image server.

We also thank the Digital Media Project (DMP) in the Department of English at The Ohio State University, which provided classroom support for the editorial team. The former director of the DMP, Professor Scott Lloyd Dewitt, generously allocated funds from the DMP to purchase a classroom license for the specialized XML-editing software used by the editorial team.

We also thank anonymous reviewers at NINES (Networked Infrastructure for Nineteenth-Century Electronic Scholarship) for their suggestions regarding the edition, and NINES for aggregating information about the edition on the NINES site. Finally, we thank Maureen Walsh and the staff of The Ohio State University Libraries' institutional repository, The Knowledge Bank, for hosting and preserving the edition.
